Some posts take longer to come to fruition than others, and I think that so far, this one is the longest!


In 2007 at the Smart Geometry Conference in New York at Cooper Union, Rob Woodbury gave a talk about the T shaped person. He was relating the concept, borrowed from..., that in order to be a good computational designer you needed to exhibit a T shaped skill profile. The idea is that you have a broad base of skills, and then a deep understanding of your chosen subject; something like having a well rounded education in everything, and a PhD in making molecular nano machines that are specifically for making smiley faces out of DNA. So your broad general knowledge is the top of your T and your deep knowledge of tiny factories is the leg.
The idea was (and I must apologise to Rob if I misrepresent him; it was a very long time ago) that you needed to know a fair bit about being an architect, and a hell of a lot about being a programmer. At the time I thought that this argument had a lot going for it, but then eventually something started niggling at me - it was the fact that Rob wasn't T shaped, and neither were any of the other people who I thought were useful and interesting.
