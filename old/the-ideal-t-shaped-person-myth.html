My first introduction to the idea of the 'T' shaped person was about three years ago. Rob Woodbury was talking at the 2007 SG conference in New York. I've thought that it was a great metaphor for describing people, a kind of personality synaesthesia. Being the obtuse weirdo I am I started to think about other shapes of people.

The obvious things that come to mind are the thickness of the top of the T, or if the leg gets really pointy at the end. Then I pretty much forgot about it for a while.

Recently I was reading a book about mountaineering, and the section on what to if there is lightening was interesting.
